# Ice Empires — Independents, Feeders & Affiliate Influence

**Date:** 2026-06-29  
**Version:** 0.4  
**Status:** Definitive design direction. Map-marker layer is **partially implemented** (see §1.1); the relationship/feeder/affiliate machinery is still target design.  
**Purpose:** Define how neutral hockey ecosystems (“Independents”) work on the map, how clubs discover and influence them, and how they become feeders or affiliates without turning Ice Empires into a conventional conquest game.

---

## 1. Design summary

In **Ice Empires**, Independents are neutral hockey ecosystems on the map. They are not rival clubs, not empty terrain, and not individual players.

They represent places, institutions, cultures, and local hockey networks that can produce players, staff, knowledge, reputation, and development opportunities.

Design examples for the future relationship/feeder layer:

- Frozen Suburb
- Prairie Rink Belt
- Finnish Goalie Lakes
- Ontario Prospect Basin
- NCAA Campus Cluster
- Maritime Grit Coast
- Desert Expansion Zone
- Swedish Development Coast
- Baltic Mystery Ice

These are not the names currently generated by the game. In code today, Independents are generated as real-world hockey city / organization labels such as **Barrie**, **Yaroslavl**, and **Iserlohn**.

The player does not conquer Independents through military combat. The player builds relationships with them through discovery, scouting, reputation, recruiting, investment, tournaments, development programs, and affiliate agreements.

Short version:

> **Independents are the city-state equivalent of Ice Empires. They are contested hockey ecosystems that can become feeders, pipelines, or affiliates.**

### 1.1 Current implementation in code

As of the current codebase, Independents already exist as generated world markers:

- Type: `WorldHockeyOrg` in `src/types/game.ts`.
- Generator: `generateHockeyOrgs` in `src/engine/world.ts`.
- Count: `HOCKEY_ORG_COUNT = 10` per generated world.
- Placement: valid land tiles only; excludes water, mountains, lakes, and mesa landforms; at least 7 tiles from the player start and 9 tiles from each other Independent.
- Archetypes: `minor-club`, `junior-league`, `rink-society`, `academy`.
- Discovery: moving a scout adjacent to an Independent sets `discovered: true`.
- State currently stored: `discovered: boolean`.
- Not implemented yet: separate contact state, first-contact rewards, Scouting Coverage, Relationship Influence, Network Level, feeder status, affiliate status, talent windows, rival contesting, and relationship decay.

## 2. Why this system exists

Most 4X games have an expansion layer where the player spreads across the map by founding cities or conquering territory.

Ice Empires should not become “settle six hockey cities.” A single club should not behave like a nation-state founding endless new capitals.

Instead, expansion should feel like a hockey organization spreading its reach:

- Scouts discover player-producing regions.
- Recruiters build trust.
- Development staff run clinics.
- Clubs create feeder relationships.
- Affiliates deepen player development.
- Rivals compete for the same pipelines.
- Local ecosystems can shift allegiance over time.

The empire is made of relationships, rinks, pipelines, influence, and player development — not conquered land.

## 3. Core terminology

### Independent

A neutral hockey ecosystem on the map. It can be discovered, scouted, influenced, contested, networked, and eventually affiliated.

### First Contact

The first time a club discovers or formally contacts an Independent. First contact gives a one-time bonus and a relationship head start, but it does not permanently lock the Independent.

### Scouting Coverage

How much a club knows about the Independent.

Scouting Coverage reveals:

- Region details
- Player output types
- Staff opportunities
- Hidden traits
- Talent cycle timing
- Rival presence
- Recruitment risk
- Development potential

### Relationship / Influence

How strongly the Independent currently leans toward a club.

This represents trust, familiarity, local buy-in, reputation, funding, and organizational presence.

### Network Level

A club’s relationship tier with an Independent. This gates what the club can do there.

### Feeder

An Independent that regularly sends player/staff opportunities toward a club. A feeder is a strong relationship but not necessarily a formal organizational affiliate.

### Affiliate

A deeper formal relationship that gives a club first access, better development slots, and stronger resistance against rival poaching.

### Contested

An Independent is contested when multiple clubs have meaningful influence there and no club has stable control.

## 4. Independent lifecycle

Target design: Independents progress through discovery and relationship states.

Current code: Independents only track whether the marker has been discovered.

| State | Meaning | Player-facing effect |
|---|---|---|
| Unknown | Hidden in fog | No information available |
| Discovered | Terrain/marker revealed | Player knows something is there |
| Contacted | First formal club contact | Basic report, first-contact bonus |
| Scouted | Club has meaningful Scouting Coverage | Talent/staff outputs become visible |
| Networked | Club has a functioning local relationship | Club can recruit basic talent |
| Feeder | Independent favors the club | Better access to talent, staff, events |
| Affiliate | Formal development relationship | Strong pipeline, development slots, map influence |
| Contested | Multiple clubs compete for control | Rival pressure, events, talent battles |
| Cold | Relationship has decayed | Access weakens unless reinvested |

An Independent can be both **Scouted** and **Contested**, or **Feeder** and **Contested**, depending on rival activity.

## 5. First-contact rule

The first club to contact an Independent gets a meaningful but non-permanent advantage.

First-contact rewards may include:

- One-time Reputation bonus
- One-time Hockey Knowledge bonus
- Basic Independent report
- Initial Scouting Coverage
- Initial Relationship / Influence points
- First chance at a small local event
- Flavor event in the log

Example event:

> **First Contact: Finnish Goalie Lakes**  
> Your scout found a region where every quiet kid seems to track pucks like a rumor. Arizona Monsoon HC gains +1 Reputation and initial Scouting Coverage.

First contact should feel good, but it should not become permanent ownership.

Rivals can still invest, overtake, and eventually flip the Independent.

---

## 6. Two different values: Scouting Coverage and Relationship Influence

Do not collapse knowledge and loyalty into one number.

### Scouting Coverage answers:

> What do we know about this Independent?

A club can have high Scouting Coverage but low influence. That means the club knows the region well but does not have local trust or pull.

### Relationship Influence answers:

> How strongly does this Independent lean toward our club?

A club can have high relationship influence but low Scouting Coverage. That means locals like the club, but the club still does not fully understand the talent pool.

This creates better hockey stories:

- “We know Finnish Goalie Lakes has elite goalies, but Helsinki owns the relationships.”
- “The Desert Expansion Zone loves us, but our scouting reports are still sloppy.”
- “We found the Prairie Rink Belt first, but Saskatoon has been quietly investing there.”

---

## 7. Recommended simple level system

Start with a small number of relationship tiers. Avoid a giant diplomacy spreadsheet.

| Level | Name | Requirements | Effect |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 0 | Unknown | Not discovered | No access |
| 1 | Contacted | First contact or basic visit | Basic report, small bonus |
| 2 | Scouted | Enough Scouting Coverage | View talent/staff output types |
| 3 | Networked | Relationship threshold met | Recruit common/uncommon talent |
| 4 | Feeder | Strong relationship held over time | Recruit rare talent, recurring events |
| 5 | Affiliate | Formal agreement / high investment | First shot at top talent, development slots |

The exact numeric thresholds can be tuned later.

Example placeholder thresholds:

| Level | Scouting Coverage | Relationship Influence |
|---:|---:|---:|
| Contacted | 10 | 10 |
| Scouted | 40 | 10 |
| Networked | 50 | 40 |
| Feeder | 60 | 70 |
| Affiliate | 75 | 90 + unlock requirement |

## 8. Relationship warmth and decay

Independent relationships should grow cold if neglected.

This prevents first contact from becoming permanent map ownership and creates strategic pressure.

Possible decay rule:

- Each month, if no club action, event, facility, unit, or investment supports the relationship, influence decays by 1–2 points.
- Feeder or Affiliate status decays more slowly.
- Rival pressure can accelerate decay.
- Winning tournaments or running development events can refresh relationship warmth.

Player-facing language should be flavorful, not bureaucratic.

Examples:

- “Prairie Rink Belt has not seen your scouts in months. Relationship warmth is fading.”
- “A rival showcase has pulled attention away from your pipeline.”
- “Your development clinic kept the relationship warm.”

## 9. How rival clubs contest Independents

Rivals can compete for Independents through non-military actions.

Possible rival actions:

- Send Scout
- Send Regional Scout
- Run local showcase
- Sponsor rink upgrades
- Hire local staff
- Offer affiliate agreement
- Win a tournament involving the Independent
- Spread reputation pressure
- Poach a prospect
- Undermine your relationship through diplomacy/events

An Independent becomes **Contested** when another club crosses a meaningful influence threshold.

Suggested early rule:

> If two or more clubs have 40+ Relationship Influence with the same Independent, it becomes Contested.

Suggested control rule:

> The club with the highest Relationship Influence controls feeder/affiliate status if it leads the next club by at least 20 points and has maintained that lead for 3 consecutive turns.

This creates drama without requiring a full war system.

## 10. Map presentation

Independents should be visible on the map as special region markers, not as ordinary terrain.

Current code already generates `WorldHockeyOrg` markers and persists them in `world.hockeyOrgs`. The richer marker states below are target design.

Recommended visual states:

| State | Map treatment |
|---|---|
| Unknown | Hidden under fog |
| Discovered | Marker silhouette / rumor pin |
| Contacted | Revealed marker with name |
| Scouted | Report icon / coverage ring |
| Networked | Thin club-color influence border |
| Feeder | Stronger club-color border and feeder badge |
| Affiliate | Club crest marker / affiliate badge |
| Contested | Striped or split-color border |
| Cold | Faded border / warning icon |

The player should see their influence spreading, but it should feel like hockey reach, not national borders.

Potential overlay names:

- Club Footprint
- Hockey Footprint
- Operational Reach
- Pipeline Influence
- Regional Pull

Recommended default language:

> **Club Footprint** for local developed tiles near HQ.  
> **Pipeline Influence** for Independent relationships farther away.

## 11. Talent production from Independents

Each Independent periodically produces a small talent pool.

Talent should not appear every turn from every Independent. That would become spammy.

Recommended cadence:

- Every 6–12 months, an Independent produces a small **Talent Window**.
- A Talent Window may contain players, prospects, staff, youth cohorts, or events.
- Stronger relationships reveal and access better opportunities.

Example output from Finnish Goalie Lakes:

| Opportunity | Rarity | Access required |
|---|---|---|
| Local Goalie Coach | Common | Contacted |
| Quiet Lake Goalie | Uncommon | Networked |
| Elite Goalie Prospect | Rare | Feeder |
| Generational Goalie Rumor | Legendary | Affiliate or special event |

Example output from Prairie Rink Belt:

| Opportunity | Rarity | Access required |
|---|---|---|
| Volunteer Trainer | Common | Contacted |
| Prairie Defenseman | Uncommon | Networked |
| Heavy Two-Way Center | Rare | Feeder |
| Barnstorming Tournament Invite | Special | Networked / Feeder |

## 12. Recruiting contests

When multiple clubs pursue the same talent, access should be weighted by relationship depth.

Possible simple model:

1. Talent appears in an Independent.
2. Eligible clubs are determined by Network Level.
3. Each eligible club gets a recruitment score.
4. Recruitment score is influenced by:
   - Relationship Influence
   - Scouting Coverage
   - Reputation
   - Relevant facilities
   - Relevant staff
   - Club identity bonus
   - Rival pressure
   - Player fit / personality
5. The player receives a clear report about why they are favored or not.

Example:

> Quiet Lake Goalie is interested, but Helsinki Ice Crown has deeper local trust. Increase Pipeline Influence or assign a goalie scout before the next Talent Window.

Avoid making this a black-box dice roll. Randomness can exist, but the player should understand the levers.

## 13. Units involved

Different units should support different parts of the Independent lifecycle.

| Unit | Role with Independents |
|---|---|
| Founding Group | Very early contact/inspiration near start area only |
| Scout | Discover, first contact, basic reports |
| Regional Scout | Establish Scouting Coverage and maintain reports |
| Recruiter | Build Relationship Influence and recruit talent |
| Development Envoy | Create clinics, development partnerships, youth cohorts |
| Pipeline Builder | Establish feeder/pipeline infrastructure |
| International Scout | Contact distant or special regions |
| League Delegate | Negotiate tournaments, league access, formal relationships |
| Agent Network | Later-era recruiting and prospect rights leverage |

Important early-game rule:

> Do not start the game with advanced units that imply the club is already organized. The earliest map actions should come from the Founding Group and basic Scouts. More specialized units unlock through facilities, research, and era progress.

## 14. Facility and tech unlocks

Independent systems should unlock gradually.

Example progression:

| Unlock | System effect |
|---|---|
| Scouting Rumors | Scouts can identify Independents, not just terrain |
| Scouting Reports | Basic Independent reports show output types |
| Local Recruitment | Recruiter unit unlocked; Network Level can reach 3 |
| Organized Practice | Talent can contribute more reliably to team attributes |
| Youth Development | Youth cohorts can appear from Independents |
| Regional Scouting Office | Regional Scout unit; Scouting Coverage maps |
| Recruiting Pipelines | Feeder status and pipeline infrastructure |
| Development Partnerships | Development Envoy; better growth for connected talent |
| Junior Partnerships | Affiliate level 1 unlocked |
| Player Projection | Better talent reports, hidden traits, development risk |
| Draft/Rights | Rights-based recruiting and prospect assignment |

## 15. Club identity bonuses

Club identity should matter in Independent relationships.

Example: Arizona Monsoon HC

**Nontraditional Market**

- Gains bonus Reputation from first contact with unusual/nontraditional Independents.
- Builds Relationship Influence faster in warm-weather, overlooked, low-probability, or expansion regions.
- Can develop dryland/desert hockey programs earlier than traditional clubs.

Example applications:

- Desert Expansion Zone
- Tropical Rink Experiment
- High-Desert Campus Cluster
- Overlooked Suburb
- Nontraditional Urban Ice Project

Other clubs can have their own advantages:

- Helsinki-style club: goalie/scouting bonus in cold regions.
- Saskatoon-style club: stronger Prairie/Rink Belt influence.
- Prague-style club: diplomacy/transfer network bonus.
- Stockholm-style club: development pipeline bonus.

## 16. Avoiding overcomplication

This system can easily become too deep too early.

Start with the smallest playable version:

### MVP Independent System

Already implemented:

- Independents appear on the map as generated `WorldHockeyOrg` markers.
- Each generated world gets 10 Independents.
- Names are drawn from the current hockey city / organization name pool.
- Each Independent has an archetype and discovered/unrevealed state.

Still to implement:

- Separate formal contact state beyond discovery.
- First contact gives a bonus.
- Each Independent has Scouting Coverage and Relationship Influence.
- Relationship Level gates access to talent.
- Talent appears every N turns.
- Rival influence can be mocked or scripted.
- Map border/marker shows current feeder/contested state.

Do not implement yet:

- Full rival AI
- Dozens of outpost types
- Complex treaties
- Full prospect rights
- Deep player database
- Contract negotiation
- Salary cap implications
- Multiple nested affiliate rosters

## 17. Data model sketch

Current code model:

```ts
type WorldHockeyOrg = {
  id: string;
  name: string;
  x: number;
  y: number;
  archetype: "minor-club" | "junior-league" | "rink-society" | "academy";
  discovered: boolean;
};
```

Target design model:

```ts
type IndependentState =
  | "unknown"
  | "discovered"
  | "contacted"
  | "scouted"
  | "networked"
  | "feeder"
  | "affiliate"
  | "contested"
  | "cold";

type Independent = {
  id: string;
  name: string;
  mapPosition: { x: number; y: number };
  terrainContext: WorldTerrain[];
  tags: string[];
  state: IndependentState;
  firstContactClubId?: string;
  currentFeederClubId?: string;
  currentAffiliateClubId?: string;
  scoutingDifficulty: number;
  relationshipDecayRate: number;
  talentCycleMonths: number;
  nextTalentMonth: number;
  scoutingCoverageByClub: Record<string, number>;
  relationshipInfluenceByClub: Record<string, number>;
  networkLevelByClub: Record<string, number>;
  contestedByClubIds: string[];
  possibleTalentOutputs: IndependentTalentOutput[];
  possibleStaffOutputs: IndependentStaffOutput[];
  possibleEvents: string[];
  scoutReport: string;
};

type IndependentTalentOutput = {
  id: string;
  role: "forward" | "defense" | "goalie" | "specialist" | "youth-cohort";
  ageRange?: [number, number];
  rarity: "common" | "uncommon" | "rare" | "legendary";
  requiredNetworkLevel: number;
  traitHints: string[];
};
```

## 19. UX principles

The player should always understand:

- Who found the Independent first
- How much the club knows about it
- How strongly it currently leans toward each club
- What talent/staff it might produce
- What relationship level is needed for better access
- Whether rivals are contesting it
- What action would improve the relationship
- When the next Talent Window may occur

Avoid spreadsheet presentation. Use scouting reports, map markers, relationship meters, and clear action buttons.

Good UI labels:

- Scouting Coverage
- Pipeline Influence
- Relationship Warmth
- Talent Window
- Feeder Status
- Affiliate Status
- Rival Pressure

Avoid overly abstract labels:

- City-state points
- Loyalty math
- Influence spreadsheet
- Diplomatic modifier stack

## 21. Design guardrails

- Independents are neutral ecosystems, not rival clubs.
- First contact matters, but does not permanently claim the Independent.
- Scouting Coverage and Relationship Influence must remain separate.
- Rival contesting should create drama without full combat.
- Talent access should be gated by relationship depth.
- Relationship decay keeps the map alive.
- Feeder/Affiliate status should be visible on the map.
- Do not implement the full complexity until the basic map loop is fun.
